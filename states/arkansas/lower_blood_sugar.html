Diabetes education and support that fits YOUR life?

Whether you've been recently diagnosed or have had changes to your diabetes care routine, managing diabetes can often feel overwhelming. Help reduce these feelings by joining a Diabetes Self-Management Education and Support (DSMES).
The Arkansas Department of Health wants to help connect Arkansans living with diabetes with personalized education, nutrition, and support that they need.

DSMES Programs Lead to Lifelong Benefits

You can join a Diabetes Self-Management Education and Support (DSMES) program to:

  • Improved hemoglobin A1C levels
  • Improved management of blood pressure and cholesterol levels
  • Clarity on the proper use of medication and testing routine
  • Fewer or less severe diabetes-related complications
  • Healthier lifestyle behaviors, such as better nutrition, increased physical activity, and use of primary care and preventive services
  • Enhanced self-confidence
  • Lower costly expenses related to diabetes complications

Participation in DSMES is linked to improved diabetes-related outcomes. Improve your diabetes management and problem-solving skills by learning more about:

  • Healthy eating to achieve a balanced diet
  • Being active and making it part of your life
  • Monitoring your blood sugar levels and lifestyle
  • Taking medicine how your provider prescribes
  • Lowering the risk of bad diabetes outcomes
  • Healthy coping and emotional well-being
  • Identifying problems, finding solutions, and how to take action in a way that FITS your life

Great News!

DSMES is a covered Medicare service and often covered by private health plans. The number of hours covered vary based on the individual plan, so check with your doctor or insurance company to find out if you are eligible today!

Welcome to Arkansas Department of Health's Diabetes Prevention and Healthy Lifestyle Programs.

In partnership with the HALT (Health and Lifestyle Training) team, we offer professionally guided coaching programs to help Arkansas residents live healthier lives. The Arkansas Department of Health is able to support hundreds of Arkansans to join online programs. Please provide your contact information, and a team member will follow up with program details and your potential start date.

You will be contacted by one of our team members to provide details and complete your registration. We look forward to working with you!

All information you enter on an application form is confidential. It will not be shared with anyone outside the health program, including with State of Arkansas staff. After you enroll, Arkansas public health staff may receive some information strictly to evaluate and improve the program. None of this information includes details that could be used to identify you or other specific program participants.
